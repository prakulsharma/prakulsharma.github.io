Day 1 - 19th December 2021

Today, I am on fire. I have made quite a good progress working on Medsol and ARIMA Library. Medsol is a project that I and my friend started working on, in July. The inspiration was the realisation of the gross condition of the healthcare system in some parts of India. In the current pandemic situation, there has been a rise in the number of people who are self-treating themselves with prescriptions, both OTC and UTC, without any professional consultations. There has also been a rise in the steroid intake among people, following the word-of-mouth of how effective they are against the COVID-19 strains. Little do they know that there is a reason we have doctors. A lot of people still don't know the implications of consuming medicines that were not prescribed to them directly. Listening to and following what their friends or relatives are consuming, is one of the things one should never do. Every medical prescription is based on a number of factors like gender, age and weight. Ignoring even one of them could lead to disastrous results. A surge in the number of people suffering and succumbing to Black Fungus is an evidence of what I am talking about. An illness, otherwise harmless, proved to be deadly in cases where people were either given wrong prescriptions or they took the liberty of doing that themselves, as their immunity was affected due to some harmful drug interaction or wrong dosage. So, what does Medsol do? It identifies the drugs (or salts) present in a medicine that was prescribed to you and searches for any interactions between them. It labels those interactions, if present, based on their severity levels and thereby warning patients to re-check their prescriptions. We have so far managed to deploy it for production but it still needs a lot of features. I don't want to talk about its prospects because the list will be endless (I'll anyway mention them on the Medsol page). 
Apart from that I have started studying about time-series forecasting using ARIMA models. I was able to devote some time today to develop a vanilla model that predicts values in a dataset, which honestly was super duper fun! I wish to work on it more and turn it into a generic library that uses all the ARIMA models and its variants like ARIMAX and SARIMA. 
That was all for today though, thanks for reading till the end! :)
